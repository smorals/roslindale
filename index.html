Roslindale

Font History

A modern design with classic roots, Roslindale is a timeless variable font. It lives up to the declaration made by its creator: “When in doubt, use Roslindale.”

Roslindale was created in 2017 by David Jonathan Ross. It was the second addition to his “Font of the Month Club”, his challenge to produce a new font every 30 days.

Ross was inspired by “De Vinne”, a Victorian-era font, and the serif stylings of the 1960s-1970s. The font employs decorative flourishes, varying lines, and grounded sophistication.

Roslindale’s classic influences balance well with its modern flexibility. As a variable font with several weight options, Roslindale can be applied in a variety of ways. Its versatility is well-suited for contemporary works, used in everything from publications to TV series titles.

Supported languages include:

Afrikaans, Albanian, Alsatian, Basque, Bislama, Breton, Catalan, Chamorro, Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Faroese, Finnish, Flemish, Franco-Provençal, French, Frisian, Friulian, Galician, German, Greenlandic, Hungarian, Icelandic, Indonesian, Irish, Italian, Kurdish (Latin), Ladin, Latin, Latvian, Lithuanian, Luxembourgish, Malay, Manx Gaelic, Moldovan, Norwegian (Bokmål, Nynorsk), Occitan, Polish, Portuguese, Rhaeto- Romance, Romanian, Romansh, Sami (Inari, Lule, Northern, Skolt, Southern), Scottish Gaelic, Slovak, Sorbian, Slovenian, Spanish, Swahili, Swedish, Tagalog, Turkish, Uzbek (Latin), Vietnamese, Walloon, and Welsh.
